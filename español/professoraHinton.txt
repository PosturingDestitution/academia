Conjugation refers to the modification of a verb to express different grammatical features, such as tense, mood, aspect, person, number, or voice. It involves altering the base form of the verb to align with the subject of the sentence or the time the action occurs.

In other languages, conjugation can be more complex, involving different forms for different persons (I, you, he/she/it, etc.) and numbers (singular or plural).


Conjugation is not limited to verbs and can also apply to adjectives, pronouns, or other parts of speech in certain languages.
